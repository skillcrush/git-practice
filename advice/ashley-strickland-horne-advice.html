Hi, my name is Ashley and I have some advice for you!

When you begin the program you will question everything you've done because of imposter syndrome. Don't let someone who is senior to you intimidate you into thinking you haven't learned anything from your training.

Experienced web developers have to look up things all the time. Web development is about problem-solving and not memorization. If you think you know the solution to a problem, trust your gut and try out the code in your branch. This will be a great time to work on your debugging skills.

Technology is constantly changing and you have to be able to adapt to that. Use the resources at your job, Google, boot camps and more to continue to expand your knowledge and understanding of coding. This growth mindset will also help you with your career if you have plans to advance within your chosen company.

There will be times when you may not understand an explanation of a concept in web development. Don't give up! Instead of getting frustrated and quitting, look at other resources like MDN or W3schools to help you find the answer.
