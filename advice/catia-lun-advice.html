Hi, I'm Catia and here is some advice for cub-coders.

Lion King Simba GIFfrom Lion King GIFs

Cubs are curious and often stick their noses in bees nests.

Ever looked at a string of code and asked yourself: "What is this?" Glyphs? A secret message? Something you can eat?
The curious cub stretches a pow and poke at it, changes a word here, add braces there, forget some slashes and angle brackets.
In three words: makes a mess. Don't panic! That is how you learn to code: try, try and try again.
Practice until you become a roaring lion coder!

The more code you read and write, the more familiar it becomes. Suddenly, all the bees-code flying around aimlessly become a neat aerobatic team. And you, cub-coder, are the one choreographing it.
Everything is clear and make sense. When you see a fancy button on a web page, the corresponding code string will pop up in your mind like a second thought.
Go cub-coder: discover and practice!

Even cubs need to learn to talk.

Coding is talking to the computer. If you want the computer to understand you and do what you tell it, you need to learn its language.
As every cub, you start with elementary words and expressions, adding intricate ways to deliver your messages as you grow and learn.
Master the language and, you can make your website dance!

When you learn a new language, it seems you will never be able to learn and remember all the words. You try, but the sentences don't come right. Grammar is a nightmare.
Do not despair! Concentrate on the basics first, even if it doesn't make much sense, and add to your vocabulary as you go.
Time to have a "chat" with your text editor, cub-coder!
