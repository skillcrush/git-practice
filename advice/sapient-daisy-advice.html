Sometimes stepping back is actually a step forward

I sometimes grapple with impatience when I am trying to learn a new thing. I have learned that my first pass at learning a new skill is an "exposure" experience where I fill my brain with new terms and ideas and process.

Then I need to step back and let the new information settle in my brain and come back to it for a second pass. The second pass can be an "enrichment" experience where I am able to deepen my understanding because everything isn't so new to me. And then I take a break to let that information settle into my brain and repat until I have an "integration" experience when I feel "at home" with the new material and can feel more comfortable experimenting because every step doesn't feel so daunting.

So don't be afraid to take a moment instead of just muscling through. That moment might be where your breakthoughs happen
